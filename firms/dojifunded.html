General FAQ

What is DojiFunded?
DojiFunded gives you money to trade with. You take a short test to show you can trade carefully. Pass it, and you get an account funded by us — you trade, and you keep most of the profit you make.

What is a "prop firm"?
"Prop firm" is short for proprietary trading firm: a company that lets traders use the company's money instead of risking their own. DojiFunded is a prop firm built on crypto/blockchain technology.

Why use this instead of trading on my own?
If you trade on a normal app, you risk your own savings — a losing trade is your money gone. With DojiFunded, you pay one small fee to join, then trade with our money. A bad trade can't drain your bank account, and you still earn a share of the profit when you do well.

What can I trade?
Crypto, forex (currencies), commodities (like gold or oil), indices (baskets of stocks), and individual stocks.

Do I need to be an expert?
No. But you do need to trade carefully and follow a few simple rules — mainly, don't lose too much too fast. All the rules are clear and shown upfront.

Can I use bots or AI to trade?
Yes, trading bots and AI tools will be allowed. The only thing banned is cheating the system to get unfair results.

Does DojiFunded hold my money?
No. DojiFunded does not hold any user funds — just the evaluation fee.

How is this different from a regular trading app?
Regular app: your money, your full risk. DojiFunded: our money to trade with, your risk capped at the small joining fee, and every trade is recorded on the blockchain so anyone can check all the details — unlike legacy firms.

Evaluations and Risk FAQ

How do evaluations work?
Buy a challenge account, hit the profit target while staying inside drawdown and risk limits, and you qualify for funding.

Are drawdowns equity-based?
Daily drawdown uses a high-water mark calculation — it resets at 00:00 UTC each day based on whichever is higher between your balance and equity at that moment.

Drawdown types explained:
Static — fixed from your starting balance; never moves.
Trailing — follows your account high-water mark up; never moves back down.
Smart — trails your equity until a target threshold, then the floor locks permanently.

What happens if I breach a rule?
Accounts that violate daily or max drawdown are automatically locked.

Can I run multiple accounts?
Yes, multiple evaluations at once, within platform limits.
