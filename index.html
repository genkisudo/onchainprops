Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before risking evaluation fees.

An onchain prop trading firm provides capital to traders, typically in cryptocurrency, requiring them to pass an evaluation phase by trading on decentralized exchanges (DEXs) or specific onchain protocols rather than legacy brokers.

Onchain prop firms vary in maturity, transparency, and track record — just like traditional prop firms, but with different risk factors. The key advantage is that your trades and evaluation results are verifiable on the blockchain. Firms with "Rules Verified Onchain" enforce evaluation criteria through smart contracts rather than opaque backend systems, reducing the risk of rule manipulation or payout disputes.

Before paying an evaluation fee, verify the firm's smart contract audit status, check onchain payout history using a block explorer, read community feedback on Discord or X, and confirm whether the team is publicly known. Most onchain prop firms are not regulated like traditional brokers, so due diligence is on you. Start with a smaller account size to test the payout process before committing to a larger evaluation.

Crypto payouts are usually processed directly to your non-custodial wallet via stablecoins (like USDC or USDT) on networks such as Arbitrum, Optimism, or Solana. This offers fast, transparent settlement without traditional bank delays or arbitrary holds.

Trading onchain eliminates opaque broker risk (like B-booking or platform manipulation), offers 24/7 global markets, ensures transparent execution verified by smart contracts, and provides immediate self-custody of your split payouts.

A-booking means your orders are routed to the real exchange — for example, Hyperliquid's live order books. The firm does not take the other side of your trade; your profits and losses come entirely from live market conditions. The firm earns revenue through fees and spread rather than from trader losses. A-booking is typically reserved for traders who have demonstrated a statistically validated edge.

B-booking means your orders are executed against a simulated environment that mirrors real market prices. The firm acts as the counterparty — taking the other side of every trade. Because most traders lose money, the firm retains those losses as revenue. The experience looks identical to live trading since prices replicate the real exchange, but no orders actually touch the market. B-booking is used for traders without a proven edge or sufficient trading history.

Prop firms use a graduated booking model rather than a hard binary switch. Each trader receives a composite score based on their trading history and demonstrated edge. Traders with lower scores are fully B-booked (simulated), while those with higher scores are progressively routed to live markets — for example, a score of ~70 might route roughly 50% of orders live, while a score of 90+ means fully live execution.

This lets firms manage risk accurately: capturing losses from unprofitable traders through B-booking, while A-booking genuinely skilled traders where the firm earns fees instead of absorbing losses. All traders typically start on B-book until the algorithm has enough data to assess their edge.
